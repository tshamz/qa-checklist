  • Introduction

    QA sucks but needs to be done. It's important for a number of different reasons, but most importantly it: prevents bugs from escaping to production, eliminates unnecessary tension between the business and clients, and reduces amount of future work that needs to be done.

    Doing QA takes time and should be thorough. The only thing worse than not doing QA at all is doing QA halfheartedly. While performing QA work, it's important to try assuming the mindset and point of view of the user. Think and act the way they might as you're testing. To maximize efficiency and save time, QA should be performed throughout the whole development process. Find a bug while you're working? Fix it. Don't have time? Write a comment and carry on. The depth that you test will vary based on type of changes you make. As difficulty of implementation increases, so should the degree at which you test. If any part of a process (add to cart, quickshop modal, etc.) has been altered during development, then the whole process should be tested thoroughly. Finally, if you find any problems but are unable to remedy them, or notice an area of friction that might cause problems in the future, tell someone and then document in the code.
